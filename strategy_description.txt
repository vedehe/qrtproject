Strategy Description

Core Idea
A multi-factor mean-reversion strategy combining a stable subset of 18 technical features
with three signal enhancement layers: portfolio-level temporal smoothing, volatility-group
neutralization, and true sleeve concentration. The design goal is to improve predictive
quality without adding fragile adaptive logic: retain only features with useful standalone
signal, remove weak or redundant indicators, neutralize simple volatility exposure, and
allocate capital only to the strongest cross-sectional opportunities.

Signal Construction
1. For each of 18 selected features, shift by 1 day (no lookahead).
2. Cross-sectional rank across ALL stocks (not just universe) on each day.
3. EWMA smoothing (span=3) on the ranked values to reduce noise.
4. Demean cross-sectionally and normalize to unit sum-of-abs.
5. Combine with fixed IC_IR weights (mean IC / std IC per feature, estimated on the
   available training data only).

Feature Selection and Redundancy Control
- The starting point was the full technical-feature set.
- Weak and highly redundant indicators were removed in conservative steps rather than by
  fitting a complex meta-model.
- `know_sure_thing`, `trix`, and `macd` were removed because they had weak standalone
  predictive quality and were heavily correlated with stronger trend features.
- A correlation map was built on the transformed feature signals that actually feed the
  portfolio, not on raw indicator values. Under this map, `williams_r` was the remaining
  true duplicate and was removed because it effectively duplicated
  `stochastic_oscillator`.
- Broader high-correlation families such as
  `stochastic_oscillator` / `relative_strength_index` / `commodity_channel_index` were also
  tested for more aggressive pruning, but those reductions weakened the 2015-2019 block.
  They are therefore kept, since they appear correlated but still additive in the final
  portfolio construction.

Enhancement Layer 1: Portfolio EWMA (span=5)
After combining all feature signals, a portfolio-level EWMA with span=5 is applied.
This smooths day-to-day noise while preserving slower-moving cross-sectional structure.

Enhancement Layer 2: Volatility-Group Neutralization (10 groups)
Stocks are sorted into 10 groups by their `volatility_20` percentile rank. Within each
group, the signal is demeaned. This removes broad volatility exposure and forces the
strategy to earn returns within each volatility bucket rather than from a simple bias
toward high- or low-volatility stocks.

Enhancement Layer 3: True Sleeve Concentration (top/bottom 12%)
After vol-neutralization, only stocks in the top 6% and bottom 6% of signal strength are
kept. Long and short sleeves are then normalized separately to +0.5 and -0.5 instead of
demeaning across the whole universe. This preserves sparsity and prevents tiny residual
weights from leaking back into low-conviction names.

NaN Handling
Early years contain materially more NaN values than later years. Ranking naturally ignores
NaN values. EWMA with `min_periods=1` propagates through sparse early data, and universe
filtering ensures the strategy only trades eligible stocks.

Portfolio Construction
- Dollar neutral: long sleeve sums to +0.5 and short sleeve sums to -0.5.
- Unit capital: sum(|w|) = 1.0.
- Max weight: |w| <= 0.1 per stock, enforced via iterative clipping and sleeve rescaling.
- Effective diversification: roughly 120 names on average, concentrated only in the
  highest-conviction signals.

Iterative/Vectorized Match
Both implementations match very closely (PnL correlation > 0.9999). Signal construction
ranks ALL stocks and applies portfolio EWMA on ALL stocks before universe filtering, after
which both implementations use the same sparse sleeve construction.

Performance (2005-2019 Training Period)
- Overall: Gross Sharpe 2.441, Net Sharpe 2.247, Turnover 52.0%
- 2005-2009: Net Sharpe 2.839
- 2010-2014: Net Sharpe 2.172
- 2015-2019: Net Sharpe 1.692

The final design is intentionally conservative. Improvements come mainly from removing
weak or redundant signals and fixing the portfolio construction so that only high-conviction
names receive capital, rather than from using fast regime switches or day-to-day feedback
from realized PnL. That keeps the strategy simpler, more interpretable, and more likely to
generalize into the hidden test period.
